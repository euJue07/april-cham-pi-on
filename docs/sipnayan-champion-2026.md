- **Registration:** Online (via Google Form) or walk-in (via Ticket Booth).
- **Proof:** Sipnayan ChamPIon Ticket (proof of registration).

- Participants must be **physically present** at the competition venue.
- Contestants must adhere to the **time and conduct guidelines** during the competition.

## Competition Mechanics

The competition is divided into **2 rounds**: Elimination and Final.

### Elimination Round

| | |
|---|---|
| **Date** | March 11, 2026 |
| **Time** | 1:00 PM – 3:00 PM |
| **Venue** | Room 409 |

- **Format:** Written test.
- Each contestant is given a paper to write down their longest memorized digits of π.
- The **top 5** contestants (by longest correct digits of π) advance to the Final Round.

### Final Round

| | |
|---|---|
| **Date** | March 12, 2026 |
| **Time** | 1:30 PM – 2:00 PM |
| **Venue** | EARIST – Amphitheatre |

- **Format:** Recitation with on-screen digits.
- A digit of π appears on the screen; contestant recites in sync.
- Each contestant begins with **"3.14159…"** and continues as far as they can.
- **Order:** One at a time, in a predetermined or random order.

#### Final Round – Mistakes & Deductions

- For every **incorrect digit**, an **"x"** symbol appears and results in a point deduction from the accuracy score.
- If a contestant **stops or hesitates for more than 10 seconds**, their turn ends.
- **Ranking** is determined by the final recorded number of digits (and scoring below).

#### General Rules (Both Rounds)

- Contestants are **not** immediately eliminated for a mistake but lose accuracy points.
- The competition ends when all contestants have finished their recitation.
- **Time limit:** No strict limit, but contestants must maintain a steady pace; pauses **longer than 10 seconds** are not allowed.
- **Winner:** The contestant with the **highest total score** (accuracy + fluency + pronunciation − penalties) is declared the ChamPIon.

---

## Criteria for Judging

### Scoring Rubric

| Category      | Criteria                                                    | Points    |
| ------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | --------- |
| **Accuracy**  | 1 point per correctly recited digit                         | Up to 100% |
| **Fluency**   | Pace and confidence in recitation (steady, no long pauses)   | 10%       |
| **Pronunciation** | Clear and understandable pronunciation                 | 10%       |
| **Penalty**   | −2 points per incorrect digit                               | N/A       |

**Total Score** = Accuracy Score + Fluency Score + Pronunciation Score − Penalties

### Tie-Breaker

In case of a tie, the contestant who recited π to the **furthest digit without making an error** is the winner.

---

## Rules & Regulations

1. Contestants must maintain decorum and respect judges' decisions.
2. Any external assistance (e.g. writing or whispering digits beforehand) will result in **disqualification**.
3. Spectators and other participants must remain **silent** during recitations.
4. Judges' decisions are **final** and cannot be contested.
5. The organizing committee holds the right to review and resolve any disputes fairly.
